2 · Grammatik

Pro Thema: kurze Regel, Beispiel zum Anhören und ein Mini-Test. Lösung erst nach dem Versuch öffnen.

Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Simple = Gewohnheit / immer (Signal: every day, usually, always). Continuous = jetzt gerade (Signal: now, look!, at the moment).

Every morning my dad makes coffee. — Look! It is raining.
Mini-Test: I can't come now — I ____ (to do) my homework at the moment.
Lösung zeigen
I am doing my homework at the moment. (Continuous, Signal: at the moment)

Past Simple vs. Past Continuous

Past Simple = abgeschlossene Handlung (Signal: yesterday, last night, in 2010). Past Continuous = war gerade am Laufen (while, as).

We watched a great film last night.
Mini-Test: While I ____ (to read), the phone ____ (to ring).
Lösung zeigen
While I was reading, the phone rang. (längere Handlung = Continuous, kurze = Simple)

Present Perfect

Verbindung Vergangenheit → jetzt. Form: have/has + 3. Verbform. Signal: since, for, already, yet, ever, never, just. since = Zeitpunkt, for = Zeitraum.

She has lived in Berlin since 2020.
Mini-Test: They ____ (already / to finish) the project.
Lösung zeigen
They have already finished the project.

will / going to (Zukunft)

will = spontane Entscheidung / Vorhersage. going to = Plan / Absicht oder sichtbares Anzeichen.

Look at those clouds — it is going to rain. / I think it will be sunny.
Mini-Test: "The phone is ringing!" — "OK, I ____ answer it."
Lösung zeigen
I will answer it. (spontane Entscheidung)

Passive (Passiv)

Form: be + 3. Verbform. Das Objekt wird zum Subjekt. Wer es tut = by ... (oft weggelassen).

The children plant the trees. → The trees are planted (by the children).
Mini-Test: This bridge ____ (to build) in 1890. · Somebody stole my bag yesterday. → ?
Lösung zeigen
This bridge was built in 1890. · My bag was stolen yesterday.

Conditionals (if-Sätze)

Typ I (real): if + present, will + Infinitiv. Typ II (unwahrscheinlich): if + past, would + Infinitiv. Typ III (vergangen, irreal): if + had + 3. Form, would have + 3. Form.

If it is sunny, we will go to the beach. (I) — If I had money, I would buy a bike. (II) — If she had studied, she would have passed. (III)
Mini-Test: If I ____ (to have) more time, I ____ (to learn) Spanish.
Lösung zeigen
If I had more time, I would learn Spanish. (Typ II)

Reported Speech (indirekte Rede)

Zeit eine Stufe zurück (backshift), Pronomen anpassen. Frage → Aussage-Wortstellung (kein did!), mit if / whether.

"I am tired," she said. → She said that she was tired.
"Did you see the film?" → He asked whether I had seen the film.
Mini-Test: "We will help you," they said. → They said that ...
Lösung zeigen
They said that they would help me.

Relative Clauses

who = Person, which = Sache, that = beides, whose = Besitz.

That's the woman who won the prize. · the book which I borrowed · the boy whose father is a doctor.
Mini-Test: This is the house ____ we lived in.
Lösung zeigen
This is the house which/that we lived in.

Gerund / Infinitive

Nach enjoy, like, be good at, after, before, prepositions → -ing. Nach want, would like, decide, hope → to + Infinitiv.

I enjoy cooking. · She wants to become a vet. · They are good at playing chess.
Mini-Test: I'm looking forward to ____ (to see) you.
Lösung zeigen
I'm looking forward to seeing you. (to ist hier Präposition → -ing!)

Comparison (Steigerung)

Kurz: -er / -est (cheap→cheaper→cheapest). Lang: more / most (interesting). Unregelmäßig: good→better→best, bad→worse→worst.

London is more expensive than Mainburg. — This is the best film I have ever seen.
Mini-Test: My phone is ____ (good) than yours.
Lösung zeigen
My phone is better than yours.

4 · Reading

Lies genau, markiere Schlüsselwörter, antworte auf Englisch in ganzen Sätzen. Mit 🔊 kannst du den Text auch hören.

Text 1 — A Weekend Without a Phone

Last month our class did an unusual experiment. For one whole weekend, none of us was allowed to use a mobile phone. At first, almost everyone thought it would be impossible. On Friday evening it felt really strange. I kept reaching for my pocket, but there was nothing there.

On Saturday, something changed. My family and I went for a long walk in the forest. We talked much more than usual, and my little brother taught me a card game. In the afternoon I started reading a book — a real one, made of paper — and I finished it by Sunday night.

When we got our phones back on Monday, I had over fifty messages. But honestly, none of them was important. I have decided to keep one "phone-free" afternoon every week. It makes me feel calmer and gives me more time for the people around me.

  1. Why did the class do the experiment, and how long did it last?
  2. How did the writer feel on Friday evening? Give one detail.
  3. Name two things the writer did on Saturday instead of using a phone.
  4. True or false? "The writer thinks all the messages were important." Give a reason.
  5. What decision did the writer make after the experiment, and why?
Lösung zeigen
1. To spend one whole weekend without a mobile phone; it lasted a whole weekend (Friday–Monday). · 2. He felt strange — he kept reaching for his pocket. · 3. A long walk in the forest; a card game with his brother; read a book (two of these). · 4. False — he says "none of them was important." · 5. To keep one phone-free afternoon every week, because it makes him feel calmer and gives him more time for people.

Text 2 — A New Job at the Animal Shelter

Tom had always loved animals, so when he saw an advertisement for weekend helpers at the local animal shelter, he applied at once. He was nervous before his first day, because he had never worked with so many dogs before. The manager, Mrs Patel, welcomed him with a warm smile.

Tom's main job was to walk the dogs, clean the cages and prepare the food. The work was harder than he had expected. But there was one dog, an old grey terrier called Buster, who followed him everywhere. Buster had been at the shelter for almost a year because nobody wanted to adopt an older dog.

After three weekends, Tom asked his parents if the family could give the dog a home. To his surprise, they agreed. "Volunteering was supposed to be just a hobby," Tom says, "but it completely changed my life."

  1. Why was Tom nervous on his first day?
  2. What were Tom's three main tasks at the shelter?
  3. Why had nobody adopted Buster before?
  4. True or false? "Tom's parents refused to take the dog." Give a reason.
  5. Explain in your own words what Tom means by "it completely changed my life."
Lösung zeigen
6. Because he had never worked with so many dogs before. · 7. To walk the dogs, clean the cages and prepare the food. · 8. Because he was an old dog and nobody wanted an older dog. · 9. False — "To his surprise, they agreed." · 10. Volunteering started as a hobby, but it led him to adopt Buster and gave his life a new, happier meaning.

7 · Writing

Plane kurz (Stichpunkte), schreibe in Absätzen, nutze Konnektoren (first, then, however, because, finally) und prüfe am Ende Zeiten + Rechtschreibung. ~200 Wörter.

A) Personal Email

Aufbau: Anrede (Hi Alex / Dear Alex) → Dank/Einstieg → Hauptteil (alle Stichpunkte!) → Frage → Schluss (Best wishes / Write back soon) → Name.

Linking words: first · then · also · by the way · that's why · looking forward to.

Mustertext zeigen (Email)
Hi Alex, Thanks for your email — I'm so excited that you're coming to stay with us this summer! I really can't wait to see you again. There are lots of things we could do together. First, we could go swimming in the lake near my house, which is great when the weather is hot. Then, if you like, we could visit the city centre and try some typical Bavarian food. Please bring some warm clothes for the evenings and good walking shoes, because we might go hiking one day. Don't forget your swimming things, too! By the way, how long are you planning to stay? Let me know so that I can organise everything. Looking forward to seeing you soon! Best wishes, Gioele

B) Article / Comment (Opinion)

Aufbau: Einleitung (Thema) → On the one hand, ... (pro) → On the other hand, ... (contra) → All in all / Personally, ... (eigene Meinung).

Mustertext zeigen (Comment: "Phones at school?")
Mobile phones at school are a topic that many students and teachers argue about. On the one hand, phones can be very useful. For example, students can quickly look up information, use learning apps or contact their parents in an emergency. Moreover, many lessons today use digital material. On the other hand, phones are often a big distraction. Instead of listening, some students chat or play games. In addition, phones can be used for cheating, which is a serious problem. All in all, I can see both sides. Personally, I think phones should be allowed during breaks but switched off during lessons. In my opinion, this would be a fair compromise for everyone.

C) Short story / Online review

Story: Setze ein klares Setting, eine Hauptfigur und ein Problem; nutze Past Simple + Past Continuous. Review: sage wo/was, gib eine Bewertung (I would recommend ... / The best thing was ...) und eine Empfehlung am Ende.

Mustertext zeigen (Review)
Last weekend I visited the new café in the town centre, and I really enjoyed it. The café was bright and friendly, and the staff were very helpful. I ordered a hot chocolate and a piece of apple cake. The cake was delicious and not too expensive. The only disadvantage was that it got quite crowded in the afternoon, so we had to wait for a table. All in all, I would definitely recommend this café, especially if you go in the morning. I'm already looking forward to my next visit!

Checklist last-minute

Vor der Prüfung

  • Conditionals (I / II / III) kurz angeschaut?
  • Passiv = be + 3. Verbform — sitzt das?
  • Reported speech: Zeit eine Stufe zurück, Pronomen anpassen?
  • 10 Mustersätze auswendig?
  • Anrede + Grußformel (informell und formell) gewusst?

Während der Prüfung

  • Reading: zuerst Fragen lesen, dann Schlüsselwörter markieren.
  • Mediation: auf Deutsch, sinngemäß, nur das Gefragte.
  • Writing: alle Stichpunkte abhaken, kurz planen, Absätze machen.
  • Zeit einteilen — Schwieriges markieren und später zurück.
  • Am Ende 5 Min. Kontrolle: Tenses, he/she + s, Rechtschreibung, Großbuchstaben.
Häufige Fehler: he/she/it → das -s nicht vergessen (she plays) · since (Zeitpunkt) vs. for (Zeitraum) · Reported question = Aussage-Wortstellung (kein did!) · Mediation nicht auf Englisch und nicht Wort für Wort.
